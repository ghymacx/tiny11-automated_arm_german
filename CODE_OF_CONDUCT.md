## Our Responsibilities

### Project Maintainers

Project maintainers (kelexine and approved collaborators) are responsible for:

- Clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate and fair corrective action in response to unacceptable behavior
- Removing, editing, or rejecting comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct
- Banning temporarily or permanently any contributor for behaviors deemed inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful

### Community Members

All community members are expected to:

- Abide by this Code of Conduct in all project spaces
- Report violations they observe to project maintainers
- Not retaliate against anyone who reports violations
- Accept responsibility for their actions and apologize when appropriate

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainer:

- **Email**: frankiekelechi@gmail.com
- **Discord DM**: kelexine
- **GitHub**: Private message to @kelexine

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

### What to Include in Your Report

Please include:

1. **Your contact information** (for follow-up)
2. **Names/handles** of people involved
3. **Description of incident** (what happened)
4. **Context** (when, where it occurred)
5. **Links/screenshots** (if applicable)
6. **Any witnesses** (if applicable)
7. **Impact** (how it affected you/community)

### Confidentiality

All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter. We will:

- Keep reporter identity confidential
- Not share details outside enforcement team
- Protect reporter from retaliation
- Only share information as necessary for resolution

## Enforcement Guidelines

Project maintainers will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining consequences for violations:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from project maintainers, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

**Example**: Mildly rude comment, off-topic spam, minor argument escalation

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

**Example**: Repeated minor violations, dismissive behavior, personal attacks

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

**Duration**: 7-30 days, depending on severity

**Example**: Harassment, sustained toxicity, threatening behavior

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

**Example**: Severe harassment, doxxing, sustained pattern of violations, legal threats

## Enforcement Process

### Investigation

When a report is received:

1. **Acknowledgment** (24-48 hours): Reporter receives confirmation
2. **Review** (3-5 days): Maintainers review evidence
3. **Decision** (1-2 days): Determine appropriate action
4. **Communication** (1-2 days): Notify involved parties
5. **Action** (Immediate): Implement consequences

### Appeals

If you believe you were unfairly sanctioned:

1. Email frankiekelechi@gmail.com with "Appeal" in subject
2. Explain why you believe the decision was incorrect
3. Provide additional context or evidence
4. Appeals reviewed within 7 days
5. Final decision made by project maintainer

## Special Situations

### Technical Disagreements

**Not Code of Conduct violations:**
- Disagreeing on technical approach
- Debating implementation details
- Preferring different coding styles
- Rejecting PRs for technical reasons

**How to handle:**
- Focus on ideas, not people
- Provide technical justifications
- Accept maintainer decisions gracefully
- Continue discussion in appropriate venues

### Constructive Criticism

**Acceptable:**
- "This approach might cause memory issues. Have you considered X?"
- "The documentation could be clearer about Y"
- "I found a bug in Z, here's how to reproduce it"

**Not acceptable:**
- "This code is garbage"
- "You clearly don't know what you're doing"
- "Why would anyone do it this way?"

### Disagreements with Maintainer

**If you disagree with kelexine's decisions:**

1. ‚úÖ Ask for clarification respectfully
2. ‚úÖ Provide additional context or evidence
3. ‚úÖ Accept the final decision
4. ‚úÖ Fork the project if fundamental disagreement

**Don't:**
- ‚ùå Argue endlessly in comments
- ‚ùå Demand changes or special treatment
- ‚ùå Post complaints in multiple venues
- ‚ùå Harass or pressure the maintainer
